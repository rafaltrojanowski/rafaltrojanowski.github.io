Why I switched to nvim in 2020

Recently I’ve been jumping between VSCode and Vim8 to write my code. My last try of nvim wasn’t successful. since I’ve
found that one of my mates shared his config on Github I want to give it yet another try.

For editor, I look for the key things for me are: Usability, Speed and Minimalism.

As the first impression is important, I can 100% sure say that I like what I see. Font size, colorscheme
and navigation looks exactly like it used to be with oldie Vim, but better :)

I will try to complete this post using nvim, which from the beginning does not seems easy

and now everything works as expected!

What I got?
rubocop checker, yay!

What I lost?
I was obsesively using jj to navigate. I lost it but thanks to that I can develop a new habit

These that I had to learn:
Navigation:

[Control][b] - Move back one full screen
[Control][f] - Move forward one full screen
[Control][d] - Move forward 1/2 screen
[Control][u] - Move back (up) 1/2 screen

I used tab for moving a block of the code, so whats now?

Press > to indent (shift text one ‘ shiftwidth ‘ to the right), or press < to shift left.
Press . to repeat the indent, or u to undo if you have shifted too far.
