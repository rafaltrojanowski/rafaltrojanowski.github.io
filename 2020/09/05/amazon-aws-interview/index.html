How was my experience of being interviewed by Amazon AWS

TLDR;

I didn't get this job, but it was worth to take part and spend time on preparation. So here is how it goes:

I was approached on Linked in and received a catchy message from a recruiter. I couldn’t say no. Hey, it’s Amazon. Later, I got an email that I will need to do the Codility test. I failed this test before, during recruiting to smaller companies. This time I decided to prepare well, and I got five days.

I bought a course on Udemy and started studying. I decided to learn in random order and take only one exercise a day because of my full-time job. When the weekend was approaching, the weather was getting better, and my motivation was loosening. I was thinking of giving up, but my friend motivated me to push harder. Doing it and working full time was a bit exhausting. The good fact is that in the meantime, in my inbox landed a new message from Amazon, so it kept me motivated. I liked it.

The day of the Codility challenge

I postponed it to the day before the deadline to have more time for preparation. I usually like to approach the problems in the morning, with a fresh mind. This time decided to change and do this in the evening. close to the time that studied.

How was the Codility test?

I did the first challenge in 10 minutes. It was an easy task - comparing to what I studied on Udemy (link to the course: https://www.udemy.com/course/beat-the-codility-coding-interview-in-python). The second one wasn’t much more complicated, and it took me around 15 minutes.

Third Codility challenge

I saw the graph, I've read the description and the first thought was like I gave it up. Later I started coding something. Better to do some action, but I realized that I don't fully understand the concept. So I read it. Again. And again. My mind was getting tired, but I got an idea. I split it for small chunks, and now it seemed doable. I still was not sure if it's 100 percent correct way because of my lack of advanced algorithms knowledge, but hey, better than nothing. So I wrote my thoughts as comments because I heard this is better than leave an empty editor. Then I coded a part that I was able to solve and also tried to confirm my thinking with Stackowerflow and maybe find a way on how to proceed with this challenging task. But then I had 10 minutes left, so I just polished it a bit. Time ran out. Send!

I got 67% in total. I wasn’t prepared enough to finish the last challenge, not being diving a deep with algorithms and data structures daily hurts. So I was happy with 67%.
