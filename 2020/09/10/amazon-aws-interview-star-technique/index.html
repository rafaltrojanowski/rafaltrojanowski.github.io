How was my experience of being interviewed by Amazon AWS - STAR technique

In the previous post, I shared my experience with the Codility test.

Time was passing and was focusing on o other things. I was happy that I don’t hear anything about that because I knew how much preparation I will need to put for the next stages. So I was waiting. After a few weeks, I was talking with my friend, and I mentioned this topic. And boom, next day, I got an email from Amazon, saying: You are invited to the next round - phone interview. It was one of the most exciting moments of my life. I was jumping from happiness, like a kid. I shared this great news with my close friends. I got an email with the steps on how to prepare for the next step. I had another a few days to prepare.

Like two days before this part, I started preparing seriously. I bought a book (link: https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B088YTMJXV) and started reading it with enjoyment. There was ready to use tips on how to prepare. And this book says that more people like me do that at the last minute, so I felt a bit better about it. I knew that I need to prepare by practice and that reading is not enough.

I started reading Amazon principles and thinking about how I can adapt my stories to them. It wasn’t so easy to find success stories from my experience, but among the 11 rules, the three seemed close to me. So I prepared stories in the doc. The next day, it was a day before the interview. I felt that I am short on time. Moreover, I felt a bit overwhelmed. So I took a day off and prepared my stories. I created an Excell stylesheet with all the categories and wrote a few sentences and keywords. That was a hint which I found in a book. Somehow in the middle of the day, I received a sheet from Amazon with example questions for every principle. Perfect. So I copied over my answers from my Excell and polished it a bit. I haven’t practiced it with the mirror and anything like that. I tried to remember keywords only, and don’t repeat my answers word by word.

The day of the interview.

I got up early, at least 2 hours before my interview, to have a short walk and eat breakfast before. I was a bit stressed out because I knew this is an important day for me, and I took it seriously.

I checked this tool for the call once again to be sure it works. The interviewer let me feel comfortable. After that, we jumped into their editor, and I got familiar with another coding challenge. This time, it was more difficult than expected. Somebody was watching at my moves, and this editor didn’t allow me to compile the code. I wrote some code, a bit in a hurry. There was no compiler. I started analyzing it again, and I spotted one small issue. The engineer said I should not worry about it. Okay, so I looked at my code, and I figured it out. It will not compile. Time was passing, and I didn’t feel convinced about my solution, so I asked for help. I got a tip on how to solve it, but it still was not easy. Finally, I found a solution, but I was not confident about it. I said it with no convincing in my voice. Then the engineer said the answer, and I felt that my brain does not follow this pace. I felt that there is a gap between us definitely, and at the same time, I liked this session much as a great learning opportunity.

In the middle of a 1-hour session, we went to a soft skill part and discussing my experience. I got asked about something that I hardly remember now, and there was a suggestion that I should focus on the most recent, so all my plans fucked up because my stories were a bit outdated. So I decided to improvise and talk about my current job.

I got a question, why Amazon as I am an independent contractor.
I did not have a great answer here, and my arguments were not strong enough. I could felt that.

Then I got asked about the story from the past where I had to work under a tight deadline. I got asked about how was the customer feedback at the end. Customer obsession is one of Amazon’s principles. I tried to stick to the STAR technique as much as I can, so I explained how this experience helped me to maintain better communication with the team. This answer convinced a recruiter, and he did not ask more questions, but I can say that there was a moment when I felt under the wall, and I was silent for a few seconds. What I can recommend is not to be quiet.

Overall experience was very positive, and I have to admit that there it was a learning opportunity for myself. All this STAR technique made me think in a bit different way and changed my perspective in some way. Amazon’s principles influenced me. I was also challenged and prepared to make a Codility test. I can’t say more about the next stage. It was probably my best and most challenging interview ever.

I think that as a remote worker having online interviews from time to time is beneficial, not only for the opportunity to be challenged, but also for stay humble, and obviously, learn new things.

If you want to better prepare for your interview, not only in Amazon AWS, I would like to recommend you this book:
https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B088YTMJXV

Everyone likes to listen to success stories, so this book will guarantee you to have access to the necessary knowledge.

If you are coding and you want to get better at coding non-trivial things, I would like to recommend this course:
https://www.udemy.com/course/beat-the-codility-coding-interview-in-python

Good luck with your interviews!
